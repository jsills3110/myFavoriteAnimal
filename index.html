The Wolf

Wolves are fierce and loyal. They hunt in packs. They protect the environment by controlling the population of herd animals.

Besides controlling herd populations, they also direct where large herds can roam. For example, grazing by a river makes animals vulnerable.

If animals graze by a river, they are susceptible to wolf attacks. So the herds only visit rivers to drink and then they leave.

This protects the riverbanks from excessive erosion, and it starts a whole chain of impact, all the way down to the organisms that live in the water.
